Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil

Brazil is home to approximately 10% of the world's known species, showcasing its incredible biodiversity. The country's varied ecosystems, including the Amazon Rainforest, Pantanal, Atlantic Forest, and Cerrado, support a vast array of wildlife. The Amazon alone teems with jaguars, sloths, and macaws, while the Pantanal hosts capybaras and caimans. The Atlantic Forest shelters the golden lion tamarin, and the Cerrado is home to the maned wolf and giant anteater. Conservation efforts are vital to protect these unique species and their habitats.
